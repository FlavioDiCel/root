The Andes mountains rise like a giant wall along the eastern side of Chile, covered with snow during most of the year. From the cities below, people can see their white peaks shining in the sunlight, reminding everyone how powerful and ancient nature can be.

One winter morning, a young climber named Sofia prepared to hike a trail near the mountains. The air was cold and clear, and every step she took on the path felt like a step into another world. The silence of the mountains was deep, broken only by the wind and the distant sound of melting snow.

When she finally reached a high viewpoint, Sofia looked across the endless chain of peaks stretching far into the distance. In that moment she understood why the Andes are so important to Chile — they protect the land, shape its climate, and inspire people with their beauty and strength.
